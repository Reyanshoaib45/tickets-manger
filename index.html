P1 - HIGH PRIORITY

Separate Task Board and Repair Board

Don't mix repair jobs with general tasks
Architecture UX Design
What This Means:

Right now, technicians see repair jobs mixed with general tasks like "buy coffee" or "call customer." This is confusing! We need two separate boards: one ONLY for phone repairs, and one for general tasks.

"We do not merge the task board and repair board. This will be interlinked. It will not be merged completely, but it will be interlinked. No, no. I am saying that there should be only one board of my task. Keep it separate for now. Less confusion will be created."
What Needs to Happen:
1
Create TWO different boards in the system
2
Repair Board: Only technicians can see this. Shows only phone repairs.
3
Task Board: Everyone can see this. For general tasks like ordering parts, calling customers, etc.
4
When a sales person logs in, they should NOT see the Repair Board
Real Example from Voice: If you're a sales person, you don't need to see repair statuses. If you're a technician, you don't need to see "call customer back" tasks. Separate boards = less confusion.

Part Ordering System

When technician needs a part, create a task for store person
Core Feature Workflow
What This Means:

When fixing a phone, if the technician needs a new screen or battery, they should be able to create a "shopping list" for the store person. The store person then buys it and marks it as done.

"I am a technician and I put the repair in the order parts. I put the link, put the price and selected the person. And done. So, basically, what is the function behind it? This task has been generated."
Step-by-Step Process:
1
Technician clicks "Order Part" on a repair job
2
Popup asks: Part Link (URL)? Price? Who should buy it?
3
System creates a task for the store person: "Buy iPhone 12 screen - $49.99 - Link: amazon.com/iphone-screen"
4
Repair turns yellow to show "Waiting for Parts"
5
Store person buys part, marks task done â†’ Technician gets notification
Real Example from Voice: "There, the user will be shown that who are you assigning, because it is adding the part. It is ordering for you. Right. It will put the link. According to that link, it will put the price. How much is the price in it? And it will assign to the user that it will order the part."
Important Detail from Voice:
Multiple Parts Scenario: "One more question. If a person has ordered a part for a job, for a repair, he has ordered that part. Now in the same repair, on the same job, he orders another part. One more task will be generated. Each part will be a different task."

P1 - HIGH PRIORITY

Task Board Rules & Permissions

Who can do what with tasks - Clear rules
Security Permissions
What This Means:

On the Task Board, we need clear rules like a classroom. You can give homework to someone, but you can't change their homework answers. Only they can update their own work.

"You can add tasks to his task. You can put the name of the task, put the details and add it. So, he will get a notification in the email that Azaad has generated a task for B. Even if he will delete it, I will get a notification that he has deleted the task. He can also delete the task that he has generated. Secondly, he cannot move anyone's task. He can only generate tasks for someone."
The Rules:
YOU CAN:
  • Create tasks for anyone
  • Delete tasks YOU created
  • Take someone else's task if they're sick/away
  • See task history (who did what)
YOU CANNOT:
  • Move other people's tasks
  • Delete tasks you didn't create
  • Hide who created a task
Real Example from Voice: "For example, Riyan was working. Riyan went on leave. You told me to do this on your leave. So what I will do, I will open my board. Means for example, I opened the repair. You open the ticket there, we opened the repair here. After opening the repair, the assigned list, as soon as I open it, above the user list it was written, Assigned to myself. So it will be assigned to him."
How We Test This:

P0 - CRITICAL

Technician Dashboard - See Only Your Work

Technicians should only see their assigned repairs
Dashboard User Experience
What This Means:

Imagine you're a chef. You only need to see the orders for YOUR station, not every order in the restaurant. Technicians should only see phones THEY need to fix, not everyone's phones.

"For example, there is a senior person in the board or a team lead or an owner himself. After that there are 2-3 technicians and he has given a repair to one of them. So, our concept was that he should just hold the repair and if he has to assign the other one, then he should hold it and put it in his board."
How It Should Work:
1
When Technician A logs in, they ONLY see repairs assigned to Technician A
2
Manager/Owner sees ALL repairs in a simple list (not board view)
3
If Manager wants to transfer a repair from Technician A to Technician B, they go to the repair details and change the assigned person
4
Repair disappears from Technician A's board, appears on Technician B's board
Real Example from Voice: "Here, it looks very bad. There are 10 technicians working and 10 technicians' boards are running there. 10 technicians will have 5-5 jobs. How big will that board be? And although he has nothing to do with that board. And he does not have to know the status of the repair he is not working on."
Key Insight: Managers don't need to see everyone's Kanban board. They just need a simple list: "What repairs exist? Who's working on them? What's the status?"

P2 - MEDIUM

Task Deletion Notifications

Tell people when tasks created for them are deleted
Notifications Transparency
What This Means:

If someone gives you homework and then takes it away, they should tell you "never mind, you don't have to do it anymore." Same with tasks in our system.

"Even if he will delete it, I will get a notification that he has deleted the task. He can also delete the task that he has generated."
Deletion Rules:
1
Only the person who CREATED a task can delete it
2
When a task is deleted, the person it was assigned to gets an email: "Your task '[Task Name]' was deleted by [Creator Name]"
3
Deleted tasks are removed from the board but kept in history (for records)
Real Example from Voice: This came from the concern that if someone creates a task for you and then deletes it without telling you, you might keep looking for it or think you still need to do it.

P2 - MEDIUM

Delivery Status via Invoice System

Mark repairs as delivered from invoice, not repair board
Integration Billing
What This Means:

When a phone is fixed and ready, the "delivered" status should be handled when the customer pays and collects it (invoice system), not from the repair dashboard.

"The status of the delivery will be generated from the invoice. And what you have to do for that, you don't have to do for that. The invoice page. Whatever status is running there."
Delivery Process:
1
Phone fixed â†’ Shows in "Fixed" section on repair board
2
Customer comes to collect â†’ Sales person creates invoice
3
On invoice page, button "Mark as Delivered & Notify Customer"
4
Customer gets "Thanks for coming" email, repair moves to archive
5
Fixed repairs automatically removed from board after 1 day (cleanup)
Real Example from Voice: "The unprocessed repairs will be in the dashboard, no matter what the date is. The parts will be in the dashboard, no matter what the date is. The parts that have been fixed will be shown only according to the deal. The parts that have been fixed today will not be shown in the next day."
Auto-Cleanup: Fixed repairs should automatically disappear from the technician's dashboard after 1 day (or at night) to keep it clean.
